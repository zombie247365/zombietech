The subletting risk — why it matters

The deal-breaker risk
Most SA commercial leases prohibit the lessee from subletting, allowing third-party occupation, or parting with possession without the landlord's written consent. A zombie operator using the kitchen could be characterised as exactly this. If discovered: breach notice → potential lease cancellation → site owner loses their entire business. ZombieTech could also be named in the litigation.
SA common law key test: did possession transfer? A sublease transfers possession and requires consent. A limited license to use does not transfer possession and may not. ZombieTech's contract must be structured as a license — never a sublease.
